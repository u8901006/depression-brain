# Depression Research Journal & PubMed Search Toolkit

**Scope:** Medical, neuroscience, psychology, psychiatry, epidemiology, public health, and social science journals relevant to depression research.

**Use case:** Literature review, grant preparation, clinical research scouting, systematic search planning, and topic monitoring in PubMed.

**Core MeSH terms:**  
`Depressive Disorder`, `Depression`, `Major Depressive Disorder`, `Treatment-Resistant Depression`, `Bipolar Disorder`, `Mood Disorders`, `Affective Symptoms`, `Suicidal Ideation`, `Anhedonia`, `Psychotherapy`, `Antidepressive Agents`, `Brain`, `Neuroimaging`, `Social Determinants of Health`.

## 13. Practical Search Workflow

1. **Start broad:** use MeSH + Title/Abstract depression terms.
2. **Add one dimension:** treatment, mechanism, population, or social determinant.
3. **Add evidence filter:** randomized trial, systematic review, cohort, qualitative.
4. **Search within key journals:** especially *Journal of Affective Disorders*, *JAMA Psychiatry*, *American Journal of Psychiatry*, *Biological Psychiatry*, *Psychological Medicine*, and *Social Science & Medicine*.
5. **Use synonyms:** MDD, depressive symptoms, mood disorder, treatment-resistant depression.
6. **For clinical use:** prefer recent reviews, guidelines, RCTs, and meta-analyses.
7. **For mechanism mapping:** combine depression with brain region/network/neurochemical terms.
8. **For social research:** combine depression with inequality, stigma, loneliness, work, family, migration, culture, and social support.

## 16. Notes

- PubMed journal tags sometimes require exact NLM abbreviations. If a search returns no results, try removing the `[Journal]` tag or searching the journal title without quotation marks.
- For non-biomedical social science journals, PubMed coverage may be incomplete. Consider supplementing with PsycINFO, Scopus, Web of Science, Sociological Abstracts, and Google Scholar.
- For systematic reviews, always document exact search strings, databases, date searched, filters, and inclusion/exclusion criteria.
- For clinical decision-making, prioritize guidelines, systematic reviews, randomized controlled trials, and high-quality cohort studies.
